## Annotation: Choices behind the copy

- **Lead with the WordPress pain on the homepage** — the marketing context says this is the #1 push factor and the language masajid use unprompted. Naming it makes the reader feel seen.
- **Pricing visible in the homepage subhead** — the financial buyer (treasurer) is the bottleneck for most masajid; surfacing $49/mo above the fold removes the "is this enterprisey?" anxiety.
- **MIT / self-host repeated everywhere** — it's the strongest differentiator and the strongest objection-defuser ("what if you disappear?"). Repetition is intentional.
- **No exclamation marks, no "innovative," no "leverage"** — voice rules from the context doc. Confident-without-slick is the brand.
- **Use of Islamic vocabulary (jamaa, jummah, sadaqah, khidmah)** — signals to masjid staff that we're inside the tent. Glossed naturally so non-Muslim readers (e.g. an IT volunteer's spouse helping evaluate) don't get lost.
- **Comparison tables, not paragraphs, on `/compare`** — boards make decisions on tables, not prose.
